About the Ministry

Foundation

The Ministry of Health was established in 1921, and in 1926 the first law was issued to regulate health affairs, and this situation continued until 1939, when the Health Department of the Ministry of Interior joined until an independent Ministry of Health was established in 1950 under the name of the Ministry of Health. In 1966, the Public Health Law No. (43) was issued, and it was amended according to the Public Health Law (No. 21 of 1971). The latter was amended several times until the Public Health Law No. (47 of 2008) was issued whereby the Ministry regulates health matters in the Kingdom.

Health in the era of the emirate

We present this quick overview of the health situation before the establishment of the emirate, so that we can infer and cite the health and medical development in the period (1921-1946).

  • The first health consultant, the late Mazhar Pasha Arslan, in 1921.
  • The first director of health, Dr. Reza Tawfiq, in 1921.
  • The first government hospital was established in 1921 and the number of beds in it (20) beds.
  • The founder of the first organized health department in Jordan, Dr. Halim Abu Rahma, in 1925 and remained in this center until 1939.
  • The first law to regulate health affairs in the emirate’s era was issued by the Consultation Council under the title “A Law Relating to Hospital Doctors” in 1923.
  • On February 16, 1924, His Highness Prince Abdullah approved the list of conditions for establishing the Italian Salt Hospital. The disbursements of the Health Department for the year 1924/1925 (4991) pounds.

Historical brief about the ministry:

During the past forty years, Jordan has witnessed a comprehensive health renaissance, during which it has achieved great achievements in various medical fields, despite the severe conditions experienced by it and the suffering of the Palestinian Nakba in 1948 and 1967 and its painful health repercussions. This can be said that the emergence of a true health renaissance in Jordan came after the country's independence, the establishment of the kingdom, and the unification of the two banks. The development of a healthy renaissance in Jordan went through several stages: -

  • The first Ministry of Health was established on 12/14/1950.
  • The Ministry of Health began its duties in 1951, as this year marks the beginning of the development of the health renaissance in Jordan.
  • The Ministry’s first achievement was the establishment of (6) departments in the Kingdom’s
    • brigades, affiliated with the central administration of the Ministry.
  • The first nursing college was opened in 1953.
  • The Central Laboratory for Medical Examination was established in 1953.
  • The Doctors' Syndicate was created in 1954.
  • Princess Mona College of Nursing was established in 1962.
  • The first health insurance system was implemented in the Kingdom for members of the armed forces in 1963.
  • The Ministry implemented the first civil health insurance system in the Kingdom in 1965.
  • The Faculty of Medicine was established at the University of Jordan in 1970 The Public Health Law No. 21 of 1971 was issued, replacing the Public Health Law No. 43 of 1966.
  • Al-Hussein Medical City was opened in 1973.
  • The Institute of Allied Medical Professions was opened in Amman in 1973.
  • The Higher Health Council Law No. 60 of 1977 was issued.
  • The Institute of Medical Auxiliary Professions was opened in Irbid in 1978.
  • The Faculty of Pharmacy was opened at the University of Jordan in 1980.

The Ministry of Health is responsible for all health affairs in the Kingdom, and its tasks in particular include the following: -

  • Maintaining public health by providing preventive, curative and supervisory health services.
  • Regulating and supervising health services provided by the public and private sectors
  • Providing health insurance for citizens within the limits of its capabilities
  • Establishing educational and training health institutions and institutes affiliated with the Ministry and supervising their management, while observing the provisions of the relevant legislation in force.

The Ministry, in coordination with the relevant authorities, works to achieve the following: -

  • Encouraging and promoting healthy lifestyles and behaviors, including physical activities, adopting proper nutrition methods, encouraging smoking control, and any other patterns or behaviors that are scientifically proven to be beneficial in improving health.
  • Raising the health level of the population by combating diseases caused by malnutrition by adding micronutrients such as iodine, iron, vitamins and the like to foodstuffs or requesting modification of its components and preventing the marketing of foods that are dangerous to health and which may be at risk.
  • Encouraging and promoting breastfeeding of children, and for this purpose, it has the right to prevent any advertising, visual, audible or readable, or any way to display notes, instructions, identification cards, display sheets, pictures, films or merchandise in any form to advertise breast milk substitutes and complementary foods and control. Production, design and dissemination of information and educational materials related to it.
  • Maternal and child care by providing the necessary services for the mother and child, including caring for the pregnant woman during pregnancy, during childbirth and during puerperium, monitoring the growth of the child, and providing vaccinations, in accordance with the necessary reproductive health requirements and other health matters related to family planning.
  • Obliging those wishing to marry to conduct the necessary medical examination before marriage, and the provisions related to this examination and its conditions are defined in accordance with the regulations issued in accordance with the provisions of this law, and the marriage contract may not be conducted before this examination
  • The Ministry also works to: 1.  Providing preventive health services to school students, kindergartens, and government nurseries 2.  Providing health services and providing them as it deems appropriate for some non-governmental schools and kindergartens, or requiring their owners to provide these services under the supervision of the Ministry.
  • Implementing programs related to health activities for the care of the elderly and health supervision of their centers and institutions.
  • Overseeing the occupational environment and the health of workers in factories, factories, industrial institutions and the like to ensure their health safety.
  • Implementation of health programs and activities related to combating common noncommunicable diseases such as cardiovascular diseases, cancer, diabetes and any other similar diseases that may pose a public health risk.
