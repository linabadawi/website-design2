What is Bad Breath

Mouth odor is widespread and because of the demand of people for products or preparations to treat this problem, the store shelves are filled with mint-flavored products, solutions for mouth washing and other preparations aimed at fighting and preventing bad breath (Bad breath / Halitosis), only temporarily. Some types of food, some diseases and certain habits - are part of the factors and causes of bad breath. In many cases, bad breath can be reduced by keeping the mouth clean. In cases where simple personal care and treatment methods do not help in solving the problem, it is advisable to consult with the dentist in order to ensure that there is no more serious problem that causes stinky breath. The specific type of odor caused by stinky breath differs depending on the source or cause of the problem.

Causes and risk factors for halitosis

There are several factors that cause bad breath, including:

  • Food: residues of food crumbs accumulated in and around the teeth can cause mildew smell. Also eating foods that contain volatile fat (Essential oil) is an additional reason for breath stinking and an unpleasant smell from the mouth. Onions and garlic are the most common of these foods, but some other vegetables and spices can also cause bad breath. After eating and digesting these foods, the fats that cause severe bad breath are absorbed into the circulatory system, and from there you reach the lungs and then exhale through the breath, until they are completely removed from the body. Onions and garlic can cause self-stinking for up to 72 hours after eating them.
  • Dental problems: insufficient maintenance of dental hygiene and periodontal diseases can lead to fetid breathing. When teeth are not rubbed and cleaned with dental floss on a daily basis, food crumbs accumulate in the mouth, germs collect on it and release fumes of hydrogen sulfide (Hydrogen sulfide). Thus, a transparent and sticky layer of germs accumulates on the teeth (Plaque plaque). In the event that the teeth are not rubbed, plaques accumulate and may lead to gum excitation, to inflammation of the gums and to tooth decay. Ultimately, plaque-filled sachets may form between the teeth and gums (Periodontitis) that increase the severity of the problem and stink, both. Also the dental prosthesis (Denture teeth) that are not kept clean, well, and those that are not suitable for the mouth as it should, can constitute a hotbed of food debris that will collect germs and release unpleasant odors.
  • Dry mouth: saliva helps clean the mouth and forms a Lubricant in the mouth. As the mouth dries, dead cells accumulate on the tongue, gums, and the inner part of the cheek. These cells rot and cause bad breath. Usually, the mouth becomes dehydrated during sleep. This is why the mouth stinks in the morning. Then the dry mouth worsens when sleeping and the mouth is open. Also, some medications, smoking and disturbances in the salivary glands can lead to chronic dry mouth.
  • Diseases: Chronic infections in the lungs and abscess in the lungs can lead to a very sharp moldy odor. There are other diseases, too, such as certain types of cancer and metabolic disease (metabolic disease) that can cause a special (distinct) odor in the mouth. Renal failure can also cause mouth odor that remembers the smell of urine, while Hepatic failure may cause a fish-like smell. People with unbalanced diabetes (Diabetes) usually have a mouth-like fruity odor. Chronic backflow of gastric juice (GERD - Gastroesophageal reflux disease) can also cause self-stagnation.
  • Diseases in the mouth, nose and throat: Breath of the breath is also associated with infections in the sinuses, secretions from the nose and sinuses connecting to the back of the throat - all of which can cause an unpleasant odor in the mouth. In children with self-stagnation, the cause may be a foreign body stuck in the nose. Any small foreign object stuck in the nose can lead to continuous secretions from the nose that cause a putrid smell. Contamination of the throat may cause bad breath throughout its existence, while the odor will disappear and the infection will be cured. Bronchitis (bronchitis) and other infections of the upper respiratory tract, accompanied by a cough that produces a smell of sputum - are an additional source of stench of the soul.
  • Tobacco products: Smoking causes dry mouth, in addition to the unpleasant smell of tobacco itself. People who smoke tobacco are more likely than others to develop periodontal disease, which causes stinking breath.
  • Strict diets: People who depend on diets may have a stench that resembles the smell of fruit, due to Ketoacidosis - damage and decay of chemicals as a result of fasting.

Prevention of bad breath

In order to eliminate and prevent bad breath and stench, it is recommended to take the following steps:

  • Rubbing the teeth after eating helps get rid of the smell of the mouth.
  • Cleaning between teeth with dental floss, at least once a day, helps get rid of bad breath.
  • Rub the tongue and clean it with the prepared tools, so it helps us get rid of bad breath.
  • Cleaning the gums is one of the most important procedures to follow to get rid of bad breath.
  • Drinking more water also helps reduce or eliminate bad breath.
  • Use a toothbrush in a good and non-weary condition: It is very important to use the toolsdesigned to clean the teeth, which are in good condition and not worn to contribute to getting rid of bad breath.
  • Undergoing regular check-in with the dentist: Visiting the dentist every period helps to know the methods used and modern methods to get rid of bad breath.
  • Chewing fresh parsley or sucking on flavored mint flavored with sugar-free is one of the methods used by everyone to get rid of bad breath.
