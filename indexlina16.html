What is Dental Pain

                        Dental pain comes from the tissues (layers) of the tooth (ivory - Dentin and pulp) and
                        periodontium.

                        Toothache issued by ivory: This dental pain appears due to external factors, such as cold, hot
                        or sweet foods. Teeth pain is characterized by being short and disappearing at the moment when
                        the external factor is removed. The pain of the teeth is medium-intensity and does not
                        concentrate at a certain point, so that the injured person finds it difficult to determine the
                        source of this dental pain, if it is from the pain of the teeth in the upper or lower jaw. The
                        cause of dental pain is mostly associated with Caries / Cavity, which can be seen by looking at
                        the eye directly, or by X-ray imaging. This tooth decay treatment includes Tooth Filling.
                        Toothache goes away with completion of treatment.

                        Pain in the teeth coming from the pulp of the tooth: Pain in the teeth coming from the pulp of
                        the tooth is a subjective tooth pain, stems from an internal source, very severe, appears in
                        seizures and disappears between the seizure and the other. The seizure lasts between a few
                        minutes and up to an hour. Dental pain is more severe in this type of dental pain when eating
                        cold foods, while chewing or in the event of pressure on the affected tooth. This type of dental
                        pain can wake the infected person from sleep. It is difficult to determine the source of pain,
                        as the cold may be the main catalyst, but the cold moves from one tooth to another until it
                        reaches the affected teeth, causing the mother to have severe teeth.
                        Cause of dental pain: inflammation of the pulp after penetration of the pulp from acute tooth
                        decay. Treatment of tooth decay is the treatment of root canal therapy and dental pulp
                        extirpation. Dental pain usually resolves upon completion of treatment.
                        Tooth pain from periodontium: Toothache that originates from periodontitis is subjective pain,
                        originating from an internal source, continuous and intensifying during chewing. The location of
                        the pain is specific and concentrated in the affected tooth. The intensity of dental pain ranges
                        from medium to severe and is sometimes accompanied by a feeling that the tooth is raised in the
                        mouth and with swelling on the face. Age can identify infected by clicking on the teeth. In
                        other words: by identifying the sensitive age of the click. Cause of dental pain: contamination
                        of the support tissue, which usually stems from the voids in the pulp and in the root canals
                        (Periapical periodontitis) or from a deep pocket of the gums (Lateral periodontitis).

Dental pain treatment 

                     When the source of toothache comes from the pulp of the tooth, the treatment for dental pain is
                     by treating the root canal. When the source of the dental pain comes from the gums, the
                     treatment is curettage, washing and cleaning the pocket of the gums. There are instructions for
                     administering antibiotics, sometimes, in both of these mentioned infections. Dental pain usually
                     disappears 24 - 48 hours after starting treatment.
