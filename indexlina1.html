Specialists: Heart disease, the leading cause of death for Jordanians

Amman - Kawthar Sawalha

Four main causes ranked first in the cause of death among Jordanians in the latest publication of the Directorate of Communicable Diseases. The results of these statistics were extracted from death notification forms from the total deaths that amounted to 19,190,191. The diseases of the cardiovascular circulatory system accounted for 39.1% of all deaths. These diseases are mainly caused by high blood pressure, followed by ischemic heart diseases and cerebrovascular diseases. As for cancerous tumors, it was the second cause of death with 16.5% of all deaths. The leaflet classifies tumors as follows: lung and trachea cancer first, then small bowel cancer, colon cancer, rectal cancer, anus and breast cancer. While the third cause of death came from the external causes of death represented by traffic accidents at 8.2% of the total deaths, while the fourth reason was due to endocrine and nutritional diseases and metabolism by 8.1%, and the first reason is due to diabetes, and then followed by respiratory diseases by 0.6% and period diseases About birth 16.4%, while the remaining percentage was distributed between the urinary reproductive system, congenital anomalies, diseases of the digestive system, infectious and parasitic diseases, diseases of the nervous system in addition to blood diseases, organs of blood formation, muscular system, skin diseases, pregnancy, and postpartum in addition to the diseases Behavioral, ear, nose and throat. Specialists said that it is not surprising from these reasons, because high blood pressure and high percentage of fats, which lead to scientific imperative to atherosclerosis and acute heart attacks, which is the first cause of death in the Kingdom widely spread among Jordanians and sometimes without their knowledge of the lack of a culture of early detection, indicating that there are numbers It indicates that 36% of Jordanians suffer from high cholesterol and 49% of them suffer from high levels of fat. Doctors attribute the causes of the spread of these diseases among Jordanians in a large percentage to the stresses of life and the lack of a healthy diet and avoiding exercise and smoking in a very big way, as the numbers indicate that the smoking rate in Jordan is very high among all groups. And numerous studies have shown that 34.4% of Jordanian families suffer from a chronic disease. Chronic diseases have been identified as high blood pressure, diabetes, asthma, chest allergies, various types of heart disease, and various types of cancer. Studies confirm that high blood pressure is the most prevalent disease among Jordanians, and it constitutes 39% of the total chronic diseases, and secondly, diabetes, at 29%, while the prevalence of asthma and allergies is 7%. The figures indicate that 13% of Jordan's population suffers from chronic disease and the percentage among females is higher than that of men.
