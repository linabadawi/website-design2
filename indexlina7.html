What is What is myasthenia gravis

Myasthenia gravis is a common name for a number of diseases that harm the conductive region between the motor nerve and the voluntary muscles (Neuromuscular Synapse). When the electrical stimulus arrives, the acetylcholine (a neurotransmitter) is attached to the nerve by the nerve, where it binds to receptors in the muscle and leads to their stimulation and constriction (necrosis) of the nerve fibers. The action of acetylcholine stops when the enzyme it breaks down (Esterase) The prognosis for myasthenia is good, and most patients do their daily activities well with the help of medications. The course of the disease is not fixed, and there are periods in which the patient's condition worsens without any clear explanation.

Symptoms of Myasthenia gravis

There are several syndromes of the disease: 1) Myasthenia gravis: It is the most prevalent form of the disease, as the immune system produces abbreviations against acetylcholine receptors and leads to its destruction (i.e., a self-defeating disease). 2) Hereditary myasthenia: it occurs due to a genetic defect in one of the proteins that synthesizes the neuromuscular synapse, where its function is impaired. 3) Myasthenia from birth: It is a temporary disease in infants of mothers who are sick with myasthenia gravis, where antibody abbreviations reach them via the placenta. 4) Myasthenia in the name Eton and Wemport: it is a disease in which abbreviations are produced against calcium channels, and as a result, an imbalance in the release of acetylcholine at the tip of the motor nerve. In patients with a special type of lung cancer, this self-immune disease can also appear without malignant diseases. 5) Myasthenia due to medication: It is caused by medications that can block the passage between the nerve and muscle. There are many drugs like these, on top of which are various antibiotics. The symptoms of myasthenia gravis are: drooping of the eyelid, double vision, poor voice, difficulty swallowing and weakening of the voluntary muscles. The symptoms can be concentrated in one area, several regions, and also in all regions. These symptoms worsen during the time of physical activities, and therefore extreme fatigue is a common grumbling of muscle weakness, and these symptoms are evident at the end of the day more than the beginning. Myasthenia gravis can begin at every age, but is more common in women between the ages of 20-40. In early childhood, hereditary myasthenia is most common

Complications of myasthenia gravis

1. A muscle weakness that includes ribs of all voluntary muscles includes breathing muscles. 2. Infections due to drugs that weaken the immune system. The infection itself, exacerbates muscle weakness, such as treatment sometimes. 3. The formation of permanent muscle weakness, especially in the eyes.

Diagnosis of myasthenia gravis

Myasthenia is diagnosed by several tests: 1. Tensilon test: a substance that leads to a high concentration of acetylcholine in the synapse, by blocking the action of esterase for several minutes. The substance is injected into a vein and if there is improvement in all symptoms, we highly expect that it can be a myasthenia disease. 2. Periodic electrical stimulation test and electrical planning of one muscle fiber: These tests document fatigue or disability at the crossing between the nerve and muscle, by recording the electrical activity of the muscle, as a result of electrical stimulation of medium or high frequencies. 3. Finding anti-abbreviations (antibodies) in the blood: We find anti-abbreviations against acetylcholine receptors in 80% of patients with myasthenia gravis, in 10% of others, we can find anti-abbreviations, against a portion of the proteins associated with the future, but at 10% of Patients, antibody acronyms cannot be found.
