What is Hepatic Encephalopathy

Hepatic encephalopathy, usually in people with cirrhosis. It is reflected in the impaired functioning of the brain that is not caused by another neurological disease. Brain weakness may be reflected in a change in personality, a decline in knowledge and a low level of awareness. This disease is caused by the passage of blood directly into the brain without going through a purification process in the liver, and this disease can also affect people who do not have cirrhosis, but have another reason that prevents the purification of a large part of the blood in the liver. It is estimated that about 70% of patients with cirrhosis suffer from a certain degree of hepatic encephalopathy.

Symptoms of Hepatic encephalopathy

Hepatic encephalopathy is classified according to its severity from 0-4 where the number 0 indicates the stage at which no symptoms appear, and 4 indicates a coma even with no response to pain. Symptoms that may appear include: Smell fruit-like smell from the mouth. Changes in sleep pattern. A change in the way of thinking. Slight confusion. A tendency to forget things. Personality and mood changes. Lack of focus. Low level of judgment on matters. Handwriting deterioration, or difficulty performing accurate operations with two hands.   In the most difficult cases: Abnormal movements like shivering in the hands (asterixis). Restlessness and excessive stimulation to the point of epileptic seizures. Disorientation (Planotopokinesia). Bad behavior and severe personality changes. Slow and incomprehensible speech. Slow and unjustified movements. In the final stage: a deep coma. These symptoms may be severe as a result of a specific disorder, or they may be part of a chronic liver disease and worsen slowly.

Causes and risk factors for Hepatic encephalopathy

This disorder is caused by a disease that disrupts liver function or any other condition that causes blood to pass through the purification stage in the liver. The direct cause of encephalopathy is not really clear, but there are several theories that explain this case. Normally, the liver can convert the toxins that the body produces, or access from the outside (such as medicines for example) to non-toxic substances that the body gets rid of through the kidneys and then excreted through the urine. When the liver is infected, these toxins accumulate in the body and cause damage to various systems, in particular the brain. One of the toxic substances that accumulate in the body, and it is known that there is a direct relationship between its high level of accumulation and the appearance of encephalopathy is ammonia. Ammonia is produced by the body during the process of breaking down proteins, and with the help of the liver the ammonia turns into less toxic molecules that are excreted in the urine, which is urea. Another theory that links the occurrence of encephalopathy to the accumulation of another substance that affects the brain is GABAgamma - Aminobutyric acid - a brain-inhibiting neurotransmitter acid.   There are various conditions that increase the possibility of encephalopathy in addition to liver diseases: - Drought. A meal that contains a large amount of proteins. Disturbances in the concentration of different solutes, especially potassium, after vomiting or diuretics. Bleeding in the digestive system. Microbial contamination. Impaired kidney function Reduced oxygen level. Medicines affecting the central nervous system, such as barbiturates or benzodiazepines.

Complications of hepatic encephalopathy

Complications that appear in the aftermath of encephalopathy are dropsy of the brain and permanent brain damage to the point where ulcers occur. In addition to the increased risk of sepsis, kidney failure, respiratory failure and weak cardiovascular system.
