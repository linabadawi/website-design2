What is Gingivitis and Periodontal Disease, Gum Disease

Gingivitis, sometimes called gum disease (periodontal disease) - describes cases of bacterial build-up in the oral cavity, which may eventually lead, if not treated properly to loss. Teeth, as a result of damage to the layer covering the teeth.

Symptoms of Gingivitis and Periodontal Disease, Gum Disease

Gingivitis is in its early stages, without any specific signs or symptoms, such as pain, appearing. Even in later, more advanced stages of this inflammation, symptoms may be very few and very mild. Although the symptoms and signs that accompany gum disease are usually minimal and minor, gingivitis is accompanied, in most cases, by signs and symptoms that are characteristic of it, in particular.

Symptoms of gingivitis include:

  • Bleeding gums when brushing teeth
  • Gum redness, swelling (swelling) and increased sensitivity
  • A bad smell, or bad taste, from your mouth permanently
  • Retraction (withdrawal) of the gums
  • Deep gaps / sinuses appear between Gingiva and Tooth surface
  • Losing teeth or moving teeth
  • Changes in the location of the teeth and the shape of their confluence and adhesion to one another when the jaws are tightly closed, or changes in the location of the dental prostheses or the crown tooth.
    • Even if none of these signs are noticed, inflammation of the gums is possible, to a certain degree. In some people, periodontitis may affect part of the tooth only, as if it affects molars only. A dentist, or periodontologist, can diagnose and determine the severity of periodontitis.

Causes and risk factors for Gingivitis and Periodontal Disease, Gum Disease

    The causes of gingivitis is the formation of a layer of germs (plaques) on the surfaces of the teeth. In addition, there are reasons that can cause gingivitis, such as:
  • Hormonal changes: such as hormonal changes that occur during pregnancy, in adulthood, in menopause ("menopause") or during the menstrual cycle. These hormonal changes increase the sensitivity of the teeth and increase the possibility of infections in the gums.
  • Other diseases: Other diseases in the body may affect the position and integrity of the gums. Among these diseases: Cancer or acquired immune deficiency syndrome, which affects the immune system in the body. Diabetes, which affects the body's ability to absorb sugars in different foods, makes people with it more likely than others to risk dental infections, including gingivitis.
  • Taking some medications: Some medications may affect the integrity of the oral cavity, as some cause a decrease in saliva production. Saliva has properties and benefits that protect the gums and teeth. Certain medications, such as Convulsion medications, such as Dilantin and medications to treat pharyngitis, such as Procardia and Adalat can cause an extra, abnormal (layer) layer of gums to form.
  • Bad habits: such as smoking, may automatically damage your gums' ability to regenerate or recover.
  • Wrong hygiene habits: such as not brushing teeth or brushing teeth (dental floss) on a daily basis. These habits can facilitate gingivitis.
  • Family history: The presence of periodontal disease in the family may contribute to the occurrence of periodontitis on a genetic basis.
