What is Pneumonia

Pneumonia is an infection in the lungs, which can generate a very bad feeling. Pneumonia causes coughing, fever (high body temperature) and breathing difficulties. In most cases, pneumonia can be treated at home without the need for hospitalization. Usually, pneumonia lasts two to three weeks, and then goes away after that, automatically. But pneumonia can be very difficult for: Old people. Children and people with other diseases. In some cases, this may require hospitalization of pneumonia.

Pneumonia classifications:

Pneumonia in the community: It may affect people in the course of their normal daily life, for example during their work or study time. Pneumonia in health institutions: Some people may be exposed to pneumonia while they are hospitalized, or in a nursing home, this type may be much more dangerous, given that a person is exposed to infection while he is already suffering from Another disease.

Symptoms of Pneumonia

The symptoms of pneumonia, caused by the bacterium (Bacterium), often appear quickly. It includes: Cough: Cough is accompanied by a phlegm or mucus from the lungs. Phlegm may be rust green, or bloody. fever. Breathing difficulties. Shivering and chills, which causes stubbornness in the teeth, this phenomenon may appear only once, or more. Chest pain becomes more and more severe when coughing or during inhalation. Fast heartbeat. Feeling tired or exhausted. Nausea and vomiting. When symptoms of pneumonia are mild, doctors call this disease "mycoplasmal pneumonia" (also known as: atypical pneumonia, or: walking pneumonia, since the person with the disease can walk Without showing breathing difficulties). How do symptoms of pneumonia in children differ from adults? Symptoms of pneumonia in younger people may be different, less or less severe, and these do not always suffer from fever, coughing, but their cough is not always accompanied by sputum. As for the elderly, pneumonia may cause disturbance and confusion in the mental clarity, in addition to confusion and hallucinations, which are common symptoms, and if a person has a pre-existing lung disease, he may exacerbate the severity of the first disease. Symptoms of pneumonia caused by exposure to Virus are similar to symptoms of bacterial pneumonia.

Causes and risk factors for pneumonia

In general, the causes of pneumonia are Microorganisms known as Bacterium or Virus. Inflammation usually occurs as a result of the entry of the germ or virus into the lungs. And chronic diseases are considered one of the most important causes of pneumonia, such as: asthma, heart disease, cancer, or diabetes. At-risk group The risk of developing pneumonia is greater for: Smokers. Suffering from other diseases, especially those related to the lungs. Children under the age of one year and the elderly over the age of 65. People with weakened immune systems. People who have mental changes. Those who take medicines to reduce stomach acidity. Those who drink too much alcohol. Those who have just had a common cold or a cold. Those who are not getting enough nutrition - are suffering from malnutrition.

Complications of pneumonia

Complications from pneumonia that require hospitalization for treatment usually appear in the following groups: The elderly are over 65 years old. People with other diseases (especially: chronic obstructive pulmonary disease - COPD, diabetes - Diabetes, asthma, chronic renal failure, congestive cardic failure and chronic liver disease). Those treated in hospital for another illness in the last 12 months. Sputum or stomach juices get into their lungs (aspirating process). Who have mental changes. Those who have had a splenectomy, or that their spleen is not working as it should. Those who drink too much alcohol. Suffer from undernourishment. Suffering from a weakened immune system. People living in overcrowding with other people, such as in educational or treatment institutions, for example.
