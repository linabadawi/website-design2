Four main causes ranked first in the cause of death among Jordanians in the latest publication of the Directorate of Communicable Diseases. The results of these statistics were extracted from death notification forms from the total deaths that amounted to 19,190,191. The diseases of the cardiovascular circulatory system accounted for 39.1% of all deaths. These diseases are mainly caused by high blood pressure, followed by ischemic heart diseases and cerebrovascular diseases.

Emergency Departments

Amman - Petra

The Secretary General of the Ministry of Health for Technical Affairs, Dr. Ali Asaad, said that all emergency departments in government hospitals in the governorates of the Kingdom are at the highest readiness and receive cases naturally and as usual. He added in a statement to Petra that until this moment, no increase in emergency situations resulting from the current situation was reported, so that the births at Al-Bashir Hospital yesterday were lower than normal and recorded 29 normal deliveries and 6 cases of cesarean deliveries. He said that Zarqa Hospital had received three injuries in a traffic accident due to the prevailing weather conditions, all of which were minor cases. Asaad indicated that the Ministry provided Al Bashir Hospital yesterday with transportation means to deliver medical personnel to their work, and a number of these cadres were unable to reach the hospital due to the closing of roads to heavy snow. He pointed out that a small number of comprehensive health centers in the capital and governorates operate partially, and the rest of the centers were unable to reach their medical staff

His Majesty's comment on the health sector

His Majesty King Abdullah II affirmed that the highest priority that all parties involved in the medical sector must work on is the advancement of the health sector and addressing imbalances that prevent the provision of quality health care to citizens.

The medical sector in Jordan

The medical sector in Jordan is the most advanced in the region and has contributed to providing many Arab countries with its expertise and highly qualified and trained staff, but the royal vision for this sector is based on the necessity of continuing achievement and development to touch the Jordanian citizen with a high quality of health and treatment services.

The words of Dr. Dawood Hanania

Prime Minister Dr. Omar Al-Razzaz on Monday sponsored the launching ceremony of the first national report of maternal deaths for the year 2018, which monitors the achievements of Jordan in this field.
