What is Retinitis pigmentosa

Retinitis pigmentosa, more than 100 years ago, was called a disease that appeared as a pigment at the base of the eye, seen by ophthalmoscope. It was a common belief that the conversation was about inflammation. During the 20th century it became clear that the disease is hereditary and caused by a genetic defect. It also turned out that the disease develops blindness with age, more than any other genetic disease. There is a group of mutant genes that may cause disease, but only a portion of them, less than ten, is known to this day. Types of hereditary transmission are varied, as they may be prevalent (autosomal dominant), when one parent has the disease, or recessive (autosomal recessive), when both parents are exempt, but they carry the mutant gene, or they may be related to sex, when the mother is completely She is healthy, but transmits the disease to her male children. About half of the patients do not have family conditions, and some of them develop a new genetic mutation. The incidence of the disease in the United States is one in approximately 2,700 people. There is no cure for the disease at this point. Since the beginning of the 20th century, dozens of failed experiments have been conducted to treat the disease, beginning with the implantation of the placenta in the pilgrims of the eye and performed by Professor Filatov in Odessa, Russia, and ending with a series of treatments that have not proven effective yet, and may even cause Damages, and is being done today in Russia, Cuba, Iraq and elsewhere. There are, in contrast, three types of treatments with scientific reference:

  • Drug therapy (Diamox, Acetazolamide) in small doses, may help in certain cases when the injury is severe vision, caused by macular imbalance (maculae, the center of the retina).
  • Treatment with vitamin A at a low-concentration dose. A large-scale study demonstrated that vitamin A can delay the deterioration of vision fields in some patients.
  • Cataract surgery in certain cases.
It is clear that the validity of deciding to take one of the aforementioned therapies belongs to an ophthalmologist. These treatments are not suitable for all patients.

Symptoms of retinitis pigmentosa

In general, the initial symptoms of the disease begin to appear in the early childhood stage, as signs of night vision problems can be observed in children of five years of age. Night blindness is a general (global, absolute) symptom of the disease. The second symptom in terms of prevalence is that the field of vision has shrunk. 94% of patients complain of this. On the other hand, the effect on the severity of vision (central vision) is very diverse. There are patients with retinitis pigmentosa whose central vision is not affected until an advanced generation, on the other hand, there are those who lose their ability to see central in the third decade of their lives. The visible signs in the eyes change according to how long the disease lasts. There are no visible signs in the first decade of life in general. In the second nodes there are light spots and spots deep in the retina, and pigmented (black) points in the retina itself. Later atrophy of the retina appears and pigmentation in the form of bone particles characteristic of the disease. A common secondary complication is septic irritation in the eye bottle and cataract.

Pigmentary retinitis treatment

Serious research is continuing to treat retinitis pigmentosa, or a method for a real improvement in vision impairment. Part of the research is being done on people with the disease. The research takes three different directions:

  • Replace the affected gene. The replacement process develops by attaching a healthy gene virus and injecting them together into the eye or blood, which spreads throughout the body.
  • Transplantation of embryonic cells inside the eye to replace infected cells.
  • Implanting an electronic chip in front of or behind the retina, so that the chip absorbs light and transmits excitement (stimulation) to the optic nerve bypassing the diseased retina.
There is a good chance that one of these trends will succeed in the coming years.
